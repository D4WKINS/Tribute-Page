Introduction

Ermias Joseph Asghedom born on August 15, 1985 to Angelique Smith, an African-American woman, and his father Dawit Asghedom, an Eritrean immigrant. He was raised in the Crenshaw neighborhood of South Los Angeles with his brother Samuel and his sister Samantha. Ermias known professionally as Nipsey Hussle was a Grammy-nominated rap artist, activist, and entrepreneur.

Nipsey Hussle was shot dead outside his own clothing store in South Los Angeles where he grew up on the 31st of March 2019. He suffered multiple gunshot wounds to the head and torso, with the Los Angeles County coroner ruling the death a homicide.

Early Life

His stage name, a play on the name of comedian Nipsey Russell, originated as a nickname given to Asghedom by a childhood friend. The "Hussle" came from his entrepreneurial spirit. He shined shoes for $2.50 to pay for school clothes at age 11 with a goal of a hundred shoes a day.

Growing up in the poverty-stricken gang environment of South Central Los Angeles surrounded by mass incarceration, de-industrialization, racist police brutality, overcrowded schools and declining social services.

Nipsey got involved in the gang life by joining the Crips at a young age. Hussle attended Alexander Hamilton High School which he later dropped out before graduating because he was accused of breaking into a school computer lab.

Hussle was also arrested for petty crimes which sent him to a juvenile detention facility. Determined to change his life, he started to focus on his music career.

At the age of 19, Asghedom's father took both him and his brother Samuel on a trip to Eritrea, East Africa for three months in 2004. Asghedom credited the trip with inspiring him to become a community activist

Music Career

Through his music Nipsey Hussle inspired millions across the world. A phenomenal lyricist that delivered concise and insightful messages through words of wisdom based on his own experiences.

During his rap career, Nipsey Hussle has worked with the likes of Drake, Y.G., P-Diddy, Snoop Dogg and DJ Khaled.

Nipsey hussle released 13 mixtapes starting with his first mixtape Slauson Boy (2005), then Bullets Aint Got No Name volumes 1, 2, and 3 (2008-2009), followed by The Marathon (2010).

The Marathon Continues (2011), TMC: X-Tra Laps (2012), Raw (2012) and Crenshaw (2013) said to be the mixtape that put nipsey on the map.

Nipsey Hussle started a proud to pay campaign , 1000 physical copies of Crenshaw were made and sold at $100 each, In admiration of this movement, Jay-Z brought 100 copies and the rest were sold within 24 hours of release, profiting $100,000 in just one day.

Later on Nipsey released Mailbox Money (2014), Slauson Boy 2 (2016), Famous Lies And Unpopular Truths (2016), and his last mixtape No Pressure with Bino Rideaux (2017).

In 2018, Nipsey Hussle released his long awaited first studio album called Victory Lap. That album was nominated for best rap album at the Grammy Awards in February 2019.

Business

Hussle opened the Marathon Clothing store on June 17, 2017, which he founded along with partners Steve Carless, Karen Civil, and his brother Samiel Asghedom. Opening the store at this intersection in the Crenshaw commercial district was important to him because he wanted to invest and provide opportunities in his neighborhood of Hyde Park.

The store is billed as a "smart store," which bridges the gap between culture and technology by giving customers access to exclusive music and other content created by rappers through an app created by software engineer Iddris Sandu.

The year before his death, Hussle had bought the small shopping center where his store was located, after partnering with the real estate investor David Gross.
